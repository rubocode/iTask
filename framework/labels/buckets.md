## Buckets

* Buckets where things are stored.
* A given thing can physically reside in only one bucket.
* Lists, folders and projects are such buckets.
* A particular choice of buckets can significantly simplify managing across multiple activities.
* Lesser the number of buckets, the simpler the management.
* However, you need enough buckets to capture the essential complexity your activities.
* Your buckets become a the lens through which you see your activity.
* Keep them under a dozen, if not half a dozen.

### Business

* This is a dynamic sphere where your performance is crucial.
* **Marketing**, **Finance** and **Operations** are the primary functions of a business.
* You may decide to have three buckets dedicated to the primary functions.
* Alternatively, you may decide to go with project based buckets.
* It all depends on the specific nature of your work.
* It all depends on how you slice the cake.

### Personal

* The personal buckets tend to be a few, stable and somewhat similar between individuals.
* You may have a separate bucket for each individual in your family.
* Alternatively, you may decide to keep one bucket **Family** and use tags to help distinguish between family members.

### Social

* These buckets help you your social activity.
* You will have a bucket for your **Friends**.
* You may also have separate buckets for your religious, political or services to humanity affiliations.
